AERO_CORE

A high-tech energy company at the intersection of engineering, infrastructure, and vision. Their website needed to pass an institutional credibility test in under five seconds — and hold it indefinitely.

// 01 THE_BRIEF

Credibility is
infrastructure.

In the energy sector, your first digital touchpoint is also your first due diligence checkpoint. When institutions, partners, and investors evaluate Aero Core, the website is the opening argument. It needs to communicate authority, precision, and vision — in that order — before a single meeting takes place.

The brief demanded a site that could serve two distinct audiences simultaneously: technical engineers who evaluate capability in detail, and strategic decision-makers who evaluate positioning in seconds. Most sites fail one. We had to nail both.

// The Challenge

Complex subject, demanding audience

Energy companies fall into two traps: over-engineering the message for a technical audience, or over-simplifying it for non-technical stakeholders. The result is either a site that confuses decision-makers or one that loses the confidence of engineers. Neither was acceptable for Aero Core.

// Our Approach

Dual-audience UX with single visual language

We designed a content hierarchy where strategic narrative leads — establishing vision, capability, and market position — and technical depth follows for those who seek it. One visual system. Two user journeys. Neither audience had to compromise to get what they came for.

// 02 THE_PROCESS

Engineered
from brief.

Technical industries demand more rigorous information architecture than most. The content strategy has to be locked before design begins, and the design has to be locked before a line of code is written. For Aero Core, this was especially true — the cost of a wrong message to the wrong audience first is not recoverable.

STEP 01

Positioning Audit

We mapped the dual audience in detail: what each group looks for, in which order, and what builds their specific version of trust. The audit determined not just what content to include, but what to prioritise and what to suppress.

STEP 02

Content Architecture

A full site map defining page purpose, content priority, and cross-page linking logic. Every page was designed to work as a standalone entry point — because partners and engineers don't always start from the homepage.

STEP 03

Visual System

Industrial-strength design language: strong typographic hierarchy, precision grid, controlled use of colour for emphasis. The aesthetic communicates engineering rigour without sacrificing the kind of visual authority that attracts strategic investment.

STEP 04

Custom Development

Multi-page HTML/CSS/JS build with a shared component system for consistent header, footer, and typographic rules. Schema markup, meta structure, and Open Graph tags implemented across every page for full SEO infrastructure.

STEP 05

Cross-Device QA

Rigorous testing across viewports, browsers, and connection speeds. Institutional clients may be viewing from a boardroom TV or a mobile device in the field. The site performs correctly in both scenarios, without compromise.
